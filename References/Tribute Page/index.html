Timeline of his life

  • 1929 - born Michael King Jr. in Atlanta, Georgia
  • 1934 - father changed his name to Martin Luther King, and his sons to Martin Luther King Jr.
  • 1944 - accepted into Morehouse College, an all-male historically black college
  • 1951 - began doctoral studies in systematic theology at Boston University
  • 1953 - marries Coretta Scott
  • 1955 - Rosa Parks is arrested, and King leads the Montgomery bus boycott
  • 1958 - King is attacked and stabbed by a mentally ill woman
  • 1963 - Birmingham campaign against racial segregation and economic injustice
  • 1963 - the March on Washingon for Jobs and Freedom, where MLK Jr. delivered his "I have a Dream" speech
  • 1964 - activism in St. Augustine, Florida
  • 1965 - the Selma voting rights movement
  • 1966 - Chicago open housing movement
  • 1968 - Martin Luther King Jr, is assassinated at the Lorraine Motel in Memphis, Tennesee
